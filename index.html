About Me

As a Staff Associate II at Columbia University's Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory, I lead the ingestion of oceanographic vessel data as part of the Rolling Deck to Repository Program (R2R), ensuring the long-term preservation and distribution of environmental sensor data collected by United States academic research fleets. I have decided to pivot away from managing oceanographic data and will be joining Wesleyan University as a graduate student in Earth & Planetary Science in Fall 2025.

I also perform quality assessment and control of ship-based multibeam data for the Global Multi-Resolution Topography (GMRT) synthesis, a high-resolution global seafloor map.

I have a Bachelor of Science (BASc) in Geology/Earth Science from City University of New York City College, and a Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) in Anthropology/Archaeology from Hunter College. I have extensive geoscience research experience,including classifying soil samples, rock cores, and groundwater, deploying air monitoring equipment, and analyzing geospatial data using ESRI ArcGIS. I am motivated by data-led policy innovation to address environmental and societal issues. I am interested climate technology, policy, sustainability, and open access to scientific data for all.
